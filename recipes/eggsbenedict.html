Eggs Benedict with Hollandaise Sauce

Description

Recipe from here.

Ingredients

  • 2 English Muffins
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 slices Canadian bacon
  • Vinegar

For the Hollandaise sauce:

  • 4 Tablespoons butter
  • 4 eggs yolks
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice, or lime juice
  • 1 Tablespoon heavy whipping cream
  • salt and pepper (to taste)

Steps

For the Hollandaise sauce:

  1. Melt butter in a small saucepan. In a separate small bowl, beat the egg yolks. Mix in lemon juice, whipping cream, and salt and pepper
  2. Add a small spoon full of the melted butter to the egg mixture and stir well. Repeat this process, adding each spoonful slowly as to not curdle the eggs.
  3. Once the butter has been incorporated, pour mixture back into the saucepan. Cook on low heat, stirring constantly, for 20-30 seconds. Remove from heat and set aside. It will thickin as it cools. Stir well and add another splash of cream, if needed, to thin.

To poach the eggs:

  1. Fill a medium size pot with about 3 inches of water. Bring the water to a boil and then reduce heat until it reaches a simmer.
  2. Add a little splash of vinegar to the water. (Optional. Helps egg white to stay together in the water)
  3. Crack one egg into a small cup. Lower the egg into the simmer water, gently easing it out of the cup.
  4. Cook the egg in the simmering water for 3-5 minutes, depending on how soft you want your egg yolk. Remove the pached egg with a slotted spoon.
  5. While the egg is cooking, place the slices of Canadian bacon in a large pan and cook on medium-high heat for about 1 minute on each side.

To assemble:

  1. Toast the English muffin. Top each toasted side with a slice or two of the Canadian bacon, and then a poached egg. Top with hollandaise sauce.
